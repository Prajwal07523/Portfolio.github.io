Introduction

Rapid or instantaneous commerce, sometimes referred to as quick e-commerce, is a subset of the e-commerce market that emphasizes product delivery to clients in a short amount of time, frequently in a matter of minutes or few hours. With the growth of businesses like Blinkit, Zepto, and Swiggy Instamart—which specialize in offering speedy delivery services for groceries, household necessities, and other common items—this model has acquired a lot of support. These services use technology, data analytics, and sophisticated logistics to satisfy the rising needs of consumers for speed and convenience.

Due to consumers' growing need for rapid and easy buying experiences, the quick e-commerce business has experienced exponential growth. The global rapid commerce market, estimated by market research, is predicted to develop at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 25% from its 2022 valuation of USD 20 billion to USD 120 billion by 2030. Urbanization, rising internet penetration, and the proliferation of smartphones are major factors driving this increase.

why-portfolio

The portfolio provides an in-depth understanding of the quick e-commerce market, including its growth, trends, and competitive landscape. This knowledge is crucial for anyone looking to enter or study this rapidly evolving sector.

By identifying and analyzing business use cases that rely on data structures and algorithms, the portfolio demonstrates how theoretical knowledge can be applied to real-world problems. This bridges the gap between academic learning and practical application.

Quick e-commerce platforms face numerous operational challenges, such as real-time inventory management, efficient route planning, and personalized recommendations. This portfolio explores how innovative algorithms and data structures can optimize these processes, leading to better efficiency and customer satisfaction.
